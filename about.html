About InsightInvest

We teach the
framework,
not the tips.

InsightInvest is a financial education platform for retail investors who want to think the way institutional investors think — not just copy their trades.

The Problem

Most investment education is broken.

It teaches you what to buy, not how to think. It gives you tips, not frameworks. It builds dependence, not capability. Most retail investors underperform not because they lack intelligence — but because nobody ever taught them the actual operating system of financial markets.

We built InsightInvest to fix that. Starting with the thing that matters most: how central banks control liquidity, and how that liquidity flows into every asset price on earth.

01

Liquidity is the source of all market moves

Before you look at earnings, before you look at valuations — look at where the central banks are in their cycle. Druckenmiller has said this for 40 years. We built a whole tier around it.

02

Second-order thinking is the real edge

Most investors stop at the obvious move. Rate cut → stocks up. We ask: which stocks? Which sectors? What happens to the dollar? What does that do to EM assets? Who captures that flow?

03

Asymmetry is not optional

Never structure a trade where your max loss is greater than your expected gain. This single principle, applied consistently, changes the mathematics of your portfolio permanently.

04

Accountability makes you better

Write the thesis. Document the entry. Define the invalidation before you put capital at risk. A thesis you can't write down is a hunch you're paying tuition on.

05

Geopolitics creates the best dislocations

Every crisis creates mispricing. The Iran conflict crashed the KOSPI and spiked oil — but the asymmetric upside was in three sectors most investors ignored entirely. We find those.

Intellectual Foundations

Built on the shoulders
of the best.

InsightInvest's frameworks are drawn from the operating philosophies of the investors with the most durable, risk-adjusted track records in history.

Stanley Druckenmiller
Duquesne Capital · 30%+ annualised, never a losing year
  • Liquidity first — follow the central banks, not the analysts
  • Top-down: macro → sector → individual stock
  • When conviction is high, size accordingly
  • Never average down a losing position
  • The market is always forward-looking — buy the anticipation
Warren Buffett
Berkshire Hathaway · 20%+ annualised over 60 years
  • Buy businesses, not tickers — the owner mindset
  • Durable moat: pricing power, network effects, switching costs
  • Circle of competence — only invest in what you understand
  • Margin of safety: price significantly below intrinsic value
  • Cash in bear markets is a weapon, not a failure
