Three Choke Points and a Basket

Forget guessing which humanoid brand wins. A robot is three miracles stacked together — it has to think, it has to move, and it has to stay powered — and right now all three are supply-choked. Own the choke points, not the robot.

SECTOR VIEW: CONSTRUCTIVEInsightInvest Thematic Strategy  |  Market data as of 30 June 2026  |  For discussion — not investment advice

Below: one concentrated name for each of the three bottlenecks a humanoid robot cannot ship without, plus KOID, the single ETF ticket that wraps the broader supply chain. The thesis is deliberately brand-agnostic — whichever humanoid maker ultimately wins, every robot requires the same three inputs, so these names capture the theme without taking on single-company risk on which platform succeeds.

AMBA
The Brain
Edge-AI Vision
Ambarella, Inc. · NASDAQ
$74.74  (−23% off high)

The chip that lets a robot see and understand the world in real time, on a battery — the edge-inference layer overlooked by capital crowding into Nvidia's data-center GPUs.

Why It's the Pick
  • The pivot is complete, not aspirational. Approximately 80% of fiscal 2026 revenue ($390.7mn, +37% year-on-year) already derives from edge AI.
  • Documented robotics traction. Management reports 15+ robotics design wins across 30+ customers (names undisclosed) and $100mn-plus in cumulative robotics revenue; a named long-term agreement with Hanwha Group, worth up to $800mn over a decade, spans security, robotics, and industrial automation.
  • Provider-agnostic positioning. Regardless of which humanoid brand prevails, robots require on-device vision inference at single-digit power draw — precisely Ambarella's core competency.
The catch. The 15+ design wins are company-reported and unaudited — no customer names have been disclosed, so the figure cannot be independently verified, and the principal risk flagged by covering analysts is that design wins fail to convert into high-volume shipments. The company remains GAAP-unprofitable and trades near 80x forward earnings, meaning the edge-AI narrative is already substantially reflected in the share price. Robotics remains a modest share of total revenue today, with the bulk still derived from automotive cameras and IoT. At only 23% off its high, this name is confirming the thesis rather than trading at a discount to it.
$74.74 price · ~$3.3bn market cap · 52-wk $48.30–96.69 · FY26 revenue $390.7mn (+37%) · ~80% edge AI mix
MP
The Muscle
Rare-Earth Magnets
MP Materials Corp. · NYSE
$53.00  (−47% off high)

Every actuator requires a rare-earth magnet, and China controls roughly 90% of global supply. MP is the only rare-earth-to-magnet producer already operating at commercial scale in the United States — the direct answer to the exact constraint Elon Musk has cited as slowing his own robotics production.

Why It's the Pick
  • The only producer operating at scale today. Operates the sole major producing rare-earth mine in the US (Mountain Pass, California) and is already shipping magnets from its Independence facility in Fort Worth — record NdPr output of 917 metric tons in Q1 2026, up 63% year-on-year, with a 10x capacity expansion (10,000 metric tons) targeted for 2028.
  • Extraordinary institutional backing. The Pentagon is the company's largest shareholder (~15%), with a 10-year, 100% offtake agreement and a $110/kg price floor; Apple has committed $500mn (including a $200mn prepayment); General Motors is its first magnet customer.
  • Structural scarcity, policy tailwind. Western supply-chain de-risking is a multi-year, bipartisan priority, and MP is its flagship domestic beneficiary.
The catch. Shares trade 47% below their 52-week high and remain partly correlated to global rare-earth pricing, so volatility should be expected. The "only fully integrated" claim is itself contested — USA Rare Earth (USAR) asserts a similar position following a 2026 acquisition spree ($3.5bn in committed capital, including the Serra Verde deal) — though its mining and magnet assets remain in build-out, years from commercial scale. MP is, at present, the only producer of the two already shipping. Magnet demand also spans EVs, consumer electronics, drones, and defense; humanoid robotics is one demand vector among several, not the current driver of revenue.
$53.00 price · ~$9.4bn market cap · 52-wk $29.58–100.25 · 2025 NdPr oxide 2,599mt (+101%) · Pentagon ~15% holder
CEG
The Power
Nuclear Baseload
Constellation Energy Corp. · NASDAQ
$244.52  (−41% off high)

The robot's brain runs on compute; compute runs on electricity; and power — not chips — is increasingly the binding constraint. Constellation is the largest nuclear operator in the United States, selling round-the-clock, carbon-free baseload directly to the hyperscalers building AI infrastructure.

Why It's the Pick
  • Scale leadership. Operates the largest nuclear fleet in the US (21 reactors across 15 sites); following the Calpine acquisition, the largest private power producer globally (~55 GW).
  • Contracted hyperscaler demand. A 20-year power purchase agreement with Microsoft to restart Three Mile Island Unit 1 (now the Crane Clean Energy Center, 835 MW, online 2027) and a separate 20-year, 1.1 GW agreement with Meta at the Clinton facility, beginning June 2027.
  • Genuinely profitable today. Q1 FY2026 adjusted EPS of $2.74 (versus $2.60 consensus), revenue of $11.1bn (+64% year-on-year), EBITDA of $3.58bn, and an ongoing dividend — the only one of the three names with current earnings.
The catch. Shares are 41% below their 52-week high following a substantial 2025 rally, as the broader AI-power trade de-rated through 2026 even while underlying contracts expanded. The stock behaves like a regulated utility rather than a semiconductor name, and the marquee restart revenue does not begin flowing until 2027. Power is also two steps removed from humanoid robotics specifically, functioning more as a broad AI-infrastructure play than a pure robotics name.

The Special Play

KOID
The Basket
Whole Supply Chain
KraneShares Global Humanoid & Physical AI ETF · NYSE Arca
~$39  (+51% past 12 months)

One ticket for the entire build-out. KOID is the first US humanoid and physical-AI ETF — 57 holdings spanning semiconductors, actuators, sensors, and rare-earth magnets — offering supply-chain exposure without a single-name bet on the eventual winning robot.

Why It's the Special Play
  • Covers two of the three bottlenecks in one line. Holds the brain leg (Credo, STMicroelectronics, Renesas, Infineon, Nvidia) and the muscle leg (Harmonic Drive, Leaderdrive, Nabtesco, THK) — and holds MP Materials and Lynas directly for the rare-earth component.
  • Access to otherwise unreachable names. Provides exposure to Asian humanoid manufacturers — UBTech, Rainbow Robotics — that most US investors cannot easily access directly.
  • Diversified by design. No single position exceeds approximately 4% of the fund, mitigating concentrated single-stock risk; net expense ratio of 0.69%, approximately $261mn in assets, and a 51% return over the trailing twelve months.
