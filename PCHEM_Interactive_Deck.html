The Setup

Asia Forgot to Diversify.
PCHEM Didn't.

✦ Live
The thesis is playing out. As of March 25, 2026 — PCHEM is trading at RM5.60, up +42% from the RM3.93 entry price modelled in March 2025. Brent crude is at ~$102/bbl, crossing the bull case trigger of $100. Iran conflict has disrupted Middle East supply routes, Pengerang refinery shut its 300,000 bpd crude unit due to Hormuz feedstock shortage, and olefin/urea prices are rebounding sharply. The bull case target of RM8.20 remains live — we are currently between the base and bull scenario.

The BofA chart that's been circulating tells a brutal story. The Philippines sources 93% of its crude from the Middle East. Vietnam, 85%. Singapore, 72%. Korea, Taiwan, Thailand — all above 60%. Europe built escape routes over the past decade. Latin America doesn't need them. Asia is trapped.

But here's what the surface reading misses: not all Asian exposure is the same. Malaysia is a net energy exporter. PCHEM — Petronas's chemicals arm — sources feedstock domestically, generates RM4.59B in operating cash flow annually, and carries a RM3.81B net cash position. It's insulated from the shock in ways its neighbours fundamentally aren't.

"Every $10 rise in Brent adds approximately RM700 million to PCHEM's annual revenue. At $130/bbl — the Strait of Hormuz scenario — that's a potential RM3.4B revenue uplift from base. The margin expansion on top of that is where the real leverage lives."

The trade isn't "buy oil." The trade is: own the Malaysian petrochemical company that benefits when Asia's oil supply chain breaks. And right now, at RM3.93, the market is pricing PCHEM as if none of that ever happens.

Step 2 — Revenue Sensitivity: Oil Price to Revenue

The core mechanic. PCHEM revenue correlates with crude via two channels: (1) Product ASP channel — petrochemical product prices (ethylene, polyethylene, methanol, urea) track crude with a 60–90 day lag; (2) FX channel — higher oil weakens the Ringgit against the USD, and ~40% of PCHEM's revenue is USD-denominated, amplifying MYR-reported revenue in an oil shock.

Revenue(oil) = Base_Rev + ((oil − Base_Oil) / 10) × Sensitivity + FX_Premium = RM30,700mm + ((oil − $82) / $10) × RM700mm + oil_delta × 2.5 Where: Base_Rev = RM30,700mm ← FY2024A actual (verified, PCG press release) Base_Oil = $82/bbl ← FY2024 average Brent crude price Sensitivity = RM700mm ← per $10/bbl change (conservative estimate) FX_Premium = RM2.5mm ← per $1/bbl change via USD/MYR depreciation Floor = RM24,000mm ← bear demand-destruction floor Combined sensitivity: ~RM700–950mm per $10/bbl Conservative case uses RM700mm (lower bound of observed historical range)

Why RM700mm and not higher? FY2022 at $100+ oil saw revenue of RM29B — but that predated the Perstorp acquisition which added ~RM7B in revenue. Stripping Perstorp out and adjusting for volume growth, organic sensitivity is RM600–800M per $10. RM700M is the conservative midpoint.

Step 3 — EBITDA Margin: Spread Mechanics by Segment

Profitability depends on product spreads — the gap between selling prices and feedstock costs. PCHEM's two largest segments behave fundamentally differently in an oil shock, creating the asymmetry that makes this thesis compelling.

O&D Segment (~50% revenue)
Naphtha feedstock — priced against crude. In rising oil, product prices (ethylene, PE) reset upward with a 60–90 day lag vs feedstock costs. Net effect: initial margin expansion for 2–3 quarters. In sustained shock, spreads normalise as products catch up.
F&M Segment (~20% revenue)
Domestic piped gas at subsidised Malaysian rates. Gas feedstock costs do NOT track crude oil. When urea/methanol prices rise with the energy complex, F&M margins expand directly. Cleanest beneficiary of an oil shock — costs fixed, revenue floating.
Specialties / Perstorp (~25%)
European operations with mixed exposure. Currently dragged by impairment charges and FX translation losses (EUR vs MYR). Modelled conservatively at flat margins across all scenarios. Does not contribute to the oil shock upside.

Step 4 — P&L Bridge: EBITDA Down to PATANCI

Each rate in the bridge is backward-engineered from FY2024 audited results and cross-verified against the confirmed PATANCI of RM1,175mm.

EBITDA = Revenue × EBITDA_Margin(oil) Less: D&A = Revenue × 4.9% ← derived: EBITDA−EBIT bridge, FY2024 EBIT = EBITDA − D&A Add: Net Interest = +RM150mm/yr ← net cash → earns INCOME, not expense PBT = EBIT + Net Interest Less: Income Tax = PBT × 24% ← effective rate, derived FY2024 Less: Minority Int. = PBT × 6% ← NCI share derived from FY2024 ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────── PATANCI = PBT − Tax − NCI Verification (FY2024A): PBT RM2,161mm − Tax RM519mm − NCI RM125mm = RM1,517mm ↳ Discrepancy vs RM1,175mm confirmed = share of associates loss (~RM342mm) ↳ PPCSB / Perstorp equity losses in FY2024 account for the gap ↳ In bull scenarios, associate losses diminish as PPCSB ramps profitably KEY CORRECTION FROM v1: Original modelled RM800mm interest EXPENSE annually. With RM3.81B net cash, PCHEM earns interest INCOME ~RM150mm. Error: ~RM950mm/yr.
Step 5 — EV/EBITDA Valuation: Net Cash Applied Correctly

The direction of the net cash adjustment in the EV bridge is the most consequential technical detail in the entire model — and was the critical error in v1.

EV/EBITDA METHOD (corrected): Implied EV = Forward_EBITDA × EV_Multiple Implied Mkt Cap = Implied EV + Net Cash ← ADD cash (PCHEM is net cash positive) Implied Price = Implied Mkt Cap / 8,000mm shares Example — Base case ($85 oil): EBITDA forward = RM3,419mm EV/EBITDA mult = 9.0× Implied EV = RM30,771mm + Net cash = +RM3,810mm Implied Mkt Cap = RM34,581mm ÷ 8,000mm shares = RM4.32/share implied price v1 ERROR — treated net cash as net DEBT: Implied Mkt Cap = RM30,771mm − RM14,000mm = RM16,771mm ÷ 8,000mm shares = RM2.10/share → severely understated by RM2.22/share EV Multiple Schedule (anchored to historical + peer range): Current (March 2025): 7.9× | Base target: 9.0× | Bull: 12.5× | XBull: 16.0× Peer range: LyondellBasell 6–8×, PTT Global Chem 7–10×, Lotte 7–12×
Step 6 — P/E Cross-Check & Blended Target Price

A second valuation method runs in parallel as a cross-check. The blended target price weights both methods to arrive at a single implied price per scenario.

P/E METHOD: Implied Mkt Cap = Forward_PATANCI × P/E_Multiple Implied Price = Implied Mkt Cap / 8,000mm shares P/E Multiple Schedule: Bear: 12× ← trough; market discounts prolonged downcycle Base: 16× ← normalised; in line with KLCI industrials average Bull: 20× ← mid-cycle; earnings inflecting sharply upward X-Bull: 24× ← peak cycle; PCHEM traded 20–26× at FY2022 earnings peak BLENDED TARGET PRICE: Blended_TP = (EV/EBITDA_Price × 55%) + (P/E_Price × 45%) 55/45 weighting rationale: EV/EBITDA is more reliable for capital-intensive cyclicals because it sits above the noise of D&A, interest, tax, and one-off items. P/E provides an equity-level sanity check but is more sensitive to PPCSB distortions.
Step 7 — Model Limitations & Failure Conditions

Every model is a simplification of reality. These are the known conditions under which the model outputs would be unreliable or directionally wrong.

LIMITATION 1: Oil-to-revenue sensitivity is modelled as linear Reality: non-linear at extremes. At $130+ sustained, demand destruction may reduce volumes even as prices rise. RM700mm/per $10 likely overstates at $130+. LIMITATION 2: PPCSB / Perstorp non-cash distortions not fully modelled FX revaluation on shareholder loans and impairment charges create PAT noise. Model uses EBITDA as primary metric to sidestep this — PAT comparisons are unreliable. LIMITATION 3: Tax rate constant at 24% Deferred tax movements and jurisdiction-specific rates cause significant swings. FY2025 effective rates have deviated materially from this assumption. LIMITATION 4: FX assumed to weaken with oil If BNM raises rates aggressively in response to inflation, MYR could strengthen, reducing USD revenue translation. Partially offsets the oil upside. Not modelled. LIMITATION 5: Multiple re-rating requires a catalyst Jump from 7.9× to 12.5×+ assumes a visible, sustained earnings catalyst. Without one, multiples may stay depressed even if EBITDA improves modestly. LIMITATION 6: FY2024 baseline may be stale (as of March 2026) Q1–Q3 FY2025 data shows further deterioration. Bull thesis still intact structurally, but forward earnings starting point is lower than FY2024. Upside asymmetry is larger.
