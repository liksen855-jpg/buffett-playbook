01
The thesis — why now, why nuclear
The demand shock
AI broke the global power grid
A single ChatGPT query uses 10× the energy of a Google search. Multiply that by billions of daily AI interactions — autonomous agents, video generation, real-time inference — and you have a power demand problem growing faster than any grid was designed to handle. By 2027, four out of every ten data centers won't have enough power to operate. Solar goes dark at night. Wind stops when it's calm. You cannot run a 24/7 AI supercomputer on maybe. You need always-on baseload power. That means nuclear.
The supply crisis
14 years of deferred demand about to unwind
Utilities haven't bought uranium at replacement rate since 2012. Russia cut off. Kazakhstan just cut 2026 production guidance. U.S. mine restarts running behind schedule. The supply chain has been hollowed out over a decade of low prices. Now the IEA projects nuclear investment rising from $70B today to $210B annually by 2035. The smart money already knows — Microsoft restarted Three Mile Island. Meta signed a major power agreement with Oklo for a planned 1.2 gigawatt nuclear campus. Amazon, Google, and other hyperscalers are all pursuing nuclear deals because they know the grid cannot keep up.

"Uranium is the fuel of the AI era. And right now, supply isn't keeping up with demand."

UUUU
UUUU
Energy Fuels Inc. · NYSE American
~$18
−35% from 52-wk high of $27.90
Growth
Uranium + rare earths · Critical minerals play
The double thesis. Energy Fuels is a uranium miner and rare earth processor under one roof — the only U.S. facility capable of processing both from the same mill. That makes it a critical minerals play stacked on top of the nuclear trade. The DFARS defense deadline in January 2027 — restricting Chinese-origin rare earths in U.S. defense systems — is a hard, date-specific catalyst that most investors haven't priced.
Business model
Three segments: Uranium (conventional and ISR recovery from the White Mesa Mill), Rare Earth Elements (separation of heavy REEs including dysprosium and terbium — first-ever 99.9% purity terbium oxide from U.S. monazite feedstock achieved in 2026), and Heavy Mineral Sands (projects in Madagascar, Brazil, and Australia). The acquisition of Australian Strategic Materials adds another critical minerals dimension.
The rare earth angle most investors miss
The January 2027 DFARS deadline means U.S. defense contractors will be legally prohibited from using Chinese-origin rare earths in defense systems. There are almost no domestic alternatives at scale. Energy Fuels' White Mesa Mill is one of the only facilities positioned to fill that gap — making this a defense-critical asset, not just a mining play. Goldman Sachs highlighted this angle in February 2026.

Oklo Inc. · NYSE

Speculative
Advanced microreactor · Pre-revenue
The highest-upside, highest-risk name in the basket. Oklo builds liquid-metal-cooled Aurora microreactors for AI data centers and defense. Meta has a major power agreement with Oklo for a planned 1.2 GW nuclear campus. The commercial pipeline sits at ~14–15 GW. They're targeting reactor criticality at Idaho National Laboratory on July 4, 2026 — a binary event. If Aurora delivers on schedule, this re-rates violently.
Business model
Unique owner-operator model: Oklo builds, owns, and operates its Aurora reactors, selling power directly to customers under long-term PPAs — capturing more margin per unit than a pure design-licensing model. The 75 MWe Aurora design targets data centers and industrial facilities. Also commercialising nuclear fuel recycling (converting used fuel into usable HALEU) and isotope production. $2.54B in liquidity provides a multi-year runway.
Why the 65% dip is not a thesis break
Three factors drove the drop: (1) pure valuation compression — a $20B+ market cap for a pre-revenue company required perfection; (2) Sam Altman stepped down as chairman in April 2025 to clear potential conflicts of interest with OpenAI power deals; (3) broad risk-off rotation in early 2026. But the fundamentals improved in that window: construction started at Idaho, ~14–15 GW pipeline secured, a major Meta power agreement signed, and $2.54B in liquidity removes financing overhang. July 4 criticality is the near-term binary.

SMR
NuScale Power Corporation · NYSE
~$12
−80% from ~$50 in Oct 2025
Speculative
NRC-certified SMR design · Asset-light model
The only company in the United States with a Nuclear Regulatory Commission-certified small modular reactor design. That regulatory moat took years to build and no competitor has closed it. The stock is down 80% from its high — the hype crowd left, the technology didn't. The make-or-break question is whether TVA and Romania convert from MOUs to binding contracts before liquidity pressure forces further dilution.
Business model
Asset-light licensing model: NuScale designs and certifies its SMR modules (NRC approved 50 MWe in 2023, 77 MWe in 2025), then partners with developers like ENTRA1 and Fluor who finance, build, and operate the plants. NuScale earns design licensing fees and services revenue per module deployed. The TVA partnership (up to 6 GW across 7 U.S. states) and Romania RoPower (6 × 77 MWe, mid-2026 go/no-go) are the two near-term commercial pivots.
The bull case in numbers
Analysts project revenue quadrupling from $76M (2025) to $321M by 2028 as SMR orders materialise. If NuScale reaches $321M revenue and trades at 30× sales — consistent with high-growth infrastructure tech — the stock is worth 8–10× current levels. The caveat is timing: Romania slipped from 2027–28 to 2030, with some analysts projecting 2033–34. For this name more than any other, timeline is the entire thesis.

Risk factors — what could break the thesis
01
Uranium spot price fails to break $85/lb in H2 2026
The entire equity re-rating requires uranium prices to inflect higher and pull utility contracting off the sidelines. If spot stays range-bound between $70–85/lb, earnings upgrades stall and the valuation premium across the basket compresses. This is the single most important macro variable to watch — it sits upstream of every other catalyst in this thesis.
02
Oklo or NuScale announce construction or timeline delays
First-of-a-kind nuclear builds routinely run 2–5 years behind schedule. Any delay announcement for Aurora (Oklo) or the Romania/TVA deployments (NuScale) would trigger significant sentiment damage. The July 4 criticality date for Oklo is the nearest binary event — a miss would likely push the stock back toward $40–45 and weigh on the broader SMR narrative.
03
DOE HALEU task orders delayed beyond 2026
LEU's re-rating thesis is largely predicated on the $900M HALEU contract moving from announcement to executed task orders. Each quarter of delay extends the timeline for earnings recognition and keeps the stock range-bound. Management has described them as "imminent" for over six months — further slippage would damage credibility as well as price.
04
Equity dilution from speculative names continues
Both OKLO and SMR have either raised equity recently or signalled capacity to do so. OKLO insiders sold $39M in the past year. NuScale doubled its authorized share count. For pre-revenue companies burning cash, dilution is an ongoing headwind that can cap upside even when the thesis is directionally correct — the percentage gain required to return to a prior high increases with every new share issued.
05
Macro risk-off rotation out of energy and materials
A broad equity selloff or sector rotation away from energy and materials would hit this basket hard — particularly the speculative tier. These stocks carry high beta to general risk sentiment. Even a thesis-intact macro shock (recession fears, credit tightening, geopolitical escalation outside the nuclear narrative) could produce painful short-term drawdowns of 30–50% before the thesis plays out.
06
Geopolitical events disrupting uranium supply chains
A double-edged dynamic: geopolitical disruption can accelerate Western demand for domestic supply (bullish long-term) but causes short-term market volatility and risk-off pricing. The Q1 2026 uranium spot spike above $101/lb was partly driven by geopolitical shocks before pulling back to ~$78 — illustrating how quickly gains can reverse in a sentiment-driven commodity market.

The verdict
Lei Holdings assessment · May 2026
Selective buy — contrarian opportunity with caveats.
This is a 3–5 year thesis, not a trade.

The structural case for nuclear energy is the strongest it has been in two decades. AI-driven power demand is real and accelerating. Uranium supply is structurally constrained. Government policy is aligned. The hyperscalers are already writing billion-dollar checks. The question is not whether nuclear wins — it's when uranium prices and reactor orders inflect in ways that drive earnings. Right now, the market is mispricing that timing gap as permanent.

BWXT and CCJ are the cleanest longs: cash-flowing, visible earnings, and upside catalysts that don't require speculative leaps of faith. LEU and UUUU are for investors who believe the HALEU bottleneck and REE defense deadline are under-appreciated — both are down significantly from highs despite improving fundamentals. OKLO and SMR are call options on the frontier: sized at 2–4% of a portfolio, held for asymmetric upside, with full acceptance that they could go to zero or triple from here depending on execution.

Nobody who bought NVIDIA at $150 in 2022 regrets it. They regret not buying more. The window in nuclear and uranium is open right now — not because the narrative is hot, but because it cooled off enough for everyone who chased the headlines to leave. What's left is the thesis, the fundamentals, and the calendar of catalysts. The investors who look back at this moment in 2029 will either have been in it — or be explaining to themselves why they knew and did nothing.
