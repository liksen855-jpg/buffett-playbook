Key findings

Three things most investors miss.

Counter-intuitive findings from a full audit of 2021–2026 data across all vehicle categories.

01

The "Cheap App" Fallacy

Platforms and accounts that advertise zero fees are not free. Every vehicle charges a spread on entry and exit. A bank account with a 4–5% round-trip spread costs more over time than an ETF with a 0.30% annual fee — especially for buy-and-hold investors. Bursa Gold Dinar and TradePlus ETF are far more cost-efficient for small investors than most bank accounts appear.

02

The Shariah Compliance Mirage

Shariah labels are not permanent. Quantum Metal's ISRA certification expired April 2023 — yet marketing continues to use compliance language. Always verify against the current BNM register and active Shariah board audit, not historical marketing materials. SC Action Cease-and-desist issued March 2024.

03

Equity Over Metal

During the 2025–2026 gold surge, jewelry retailer stocks outperformed physical gold on a risk-adjusted basis. Poh Kong rose 23%. Tomei hit all-time highs of RM2.11. Their inventory appreciates with spot price while consumer demand surges simultaneously. +23% Poh Kong in late 2025–2026.

Platform risk — what happened.

Late 2023

Quantum Metal begins late investor payments. First sign of taqabudh violations — the Shariah principle requiring immediate physical settlement.

Feb 2023

HelloGold shuts down. Consumer business closed in Malaysia and Thailand. Users given less than one week to withdraw. Cites "no longer commercially viable."

Apr 2023

Quantum Metal's ISRA certification expires. Not renewed. Company continues marketing as Shariah-compliant without valid certification.

Mar 2024

Securities Commission issues cease-and-desist against Quantum Metal Exchange Inc. for offering shares to the public without registration.

2026

Bursa Gold Dinar and TradePlus ETF emerge as the two most transparent and cost-efficient retail vehicles — both regulated by Bursa Malaysia and the Securities Commission.

What to watch

The fine print nobody reads.

Every vehicle has a structural catch. These are the risks the product brochures leave out.

Quantum Metal

Regulatory and Shariah violations. SC cease-and-desist issued. ISRA cert expired. Late payments to investors. BNM explicitly states it is not licensed for gold investment products. Avoid entirely.

HelloGold

Closed since February 2023. Even physically-backed, audited platforms can fail operationally. The gold was real — the business model wasn't sustainable. Non-bank digital platforms carry inherent solvency risk.

Physical Redemption Costs

Maybank MIGA-i physical redemption costs RM25 for a 1g bar. Bank Islam BIGA-i charges RM50–RM2,000 plus mandatory Takaful. Always model full conversion costs before choosing physical redemption.

Gold Jewellery

Making charges are lost immediately on purchase. Buyback spreads run 15–25%. The companies that sell jewellery (Poh Kong, Tomei) are the better trade — they have operational leverage to the price. Jewellery is a consumption item, not an investment vehicle.

Unit Trust Fees

RHB Gold Fund charges 1.80% p.a. vs TradePlus ETF's 0.30% TER. A 1.5%+ annual drag compounds significantly over 10 years. Active gold funds rarely outperform the underlying metal enough to justify the extra cost.

ETF Premium to NAV

TradePlus Shariah Gold ETF traded at a 3.3% premium to its NAV in early 2026 — peak domestic demand. Buying at peak premium means overpaying for the same underlying gold. Monitor the premium before transacting.
