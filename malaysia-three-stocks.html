Stock № 01

Inari Amertron — the chip that passes through Penang.

KLSE : INARI · 0166
RM 1.80
MKT CAP ≈ RM 6.7 B
The business

Inari packages the RF chips inside your iPhone — via Broadcom — from Penang, the world's oldest semiconductor back-end cluster.

It's the largest pure-play OSAT (outsourced semiconductor assembly & test) listed on Bursa. Radio-frequency filters, optoelectronics, and sensor packaging are its core. Broadcom is the dominant customer; Apple is the end-buyer through that relationship.

Why the US can't replicate this

The CHIPS Act funds front-end fabrication — wafers in Arizona, Ohio, Texas. It barely touches back-end packaging, which is still ~73% Asia-Pacific. Penang has 50 years of substrate suppliers, wire-bond operators, and test houses inside a 30km radius. Rebuilding that cluster density takes a generation, not a subsidy cycle.

73%
of global semiconductor assembly & test revenue stays in Asia-Pacific. When it diversifies, it moves laterally — to Malaysia, Vietnam, the Philippines — not to the US.

Legitimate risk: Apple has been moving modems in-house. Inari's exposure to that is real but not existential — the chips still need to be packaged, and Penang is where that happens. A recent attempted Lumileds acquisition fell through on US national security grounds, leaving Inari with a clean balance sheet and RM300M+ in preserved cash.

Stock № 02

YTL Power International — Singapore's overflow, on Malaysian soil.

KLSE : YTLPOWR · 6742
RM 3.71
MKT CAP ≈ RM 32 B
The business

A 664-hectare data centre campus in Kulai, Johor — 30 km from Singapore — running Nvidia's latest Grace Blackwell GPUs and scaling to 1 gigawatt.

YTL Power is a multi-utility: it owns Wessex Water in the UK, PowerSeraya in Singapore, telco infrastructure in Malaysia, and now the region's largest emerging AI data centre cluster. The data centre thesis is what the market is paying for; the rest is stable utility cashflow underneath.

Why the US can't replicate this

This is geographic asymmetry. Singapore hosts the Asia HQs of most US multinationals, but capped its own data centre capacity at 1.4 GW in 2019. Serving Singapore-latency workloads is a physics constraint — you cannot solve it from Virginia or Texas. Johor is the only land on earth that satisfies it.

US$23B
that North American hyperscalers committed to Malaysia in the first 10 months of 2024 alone. Microsoft, Google, AWS, Oracle — all crossing the causeway out of Singapore.

YTL partnered with Nvidia on a US$4.3B project in December 2023 and completed its first Grace Blackwell facility in late 2025. The company has secured 300MW of contracted capacity and plans to add 200MW annually toward a 1,000MW target by 2031. Dato' Seri Yeoh Seok Hong's strategic moat: YTL owns its own power generation, bypassing Tenaga's grid bottleneck every competitor faces.

Stock № 03

Malayan Banking — the bank a US bank can't become.

KLSE : MAYBANK · 1155
RM 11.26
MKT CAP ≈ RM 137 B
The business

Malaysia's largest bank and, through Maybank Islamic, one of the top global sukuk arrangers in a US$4.3 trillion market the US cannot legally enter.

Malayan Banking is a top-tier ASEAN universal bank — retail, wholesale, investment banking, Etiqa insurance — with operations in 18 countries. What makes it asymmetric is underneath: Maybank Islamic is a full-service Shariah-compliant primary issuance platform. Sovereign sukuk for Malaysia and Indonesia, corporate sukuk for GCC issuers wanting Ringgit exposure, bookrunner mandates Goldman and Citi effectively subcontract.

Why the US can't replicate this

This is a legal moat, not a cost one. US banking law — the Bank Holding Company Act, FDIC deposit insurance mechanics — is built around interest-bearing structures. Islamic finance prohibits interest (riba). There is no US Shariah Advisory Council with statutory recognition. Iran is sanctioned; the GCC serves itself. Malaysia is the only English-common-law jurisdiction positioned to intermediate global Shariah capital at scale.

36%
of the global sukuk market is Malaysia, per the Securities Commission Malaysia (end-2024). Malaysia has topped the Islamic Finance Development Indicator for 13 consecutive years — ahead of Saudi Arabia, the UAE, and Indonesia.

The secondary story is simpler: Maybank is a cash-flow machine. FY2025 net profit of RM 10.5B, a 63 sen total dividend (~5.6% yield at current price), consistent mid-teen ROE, and foreign shareholding near multi-year highs above 21%. The Islamic finance leadership is the structural moat; the dividend is the reason you hold it while the moat compounds.
