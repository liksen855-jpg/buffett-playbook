Three kinds of premium

The size of a gap matters less than its cause. Read together, the seven sort into three structural buckets — and the bucket tells you whether "overvalued" is a market call or a measurement artifact.

Type 01 · Optionality

Exhibit 5 · Data integrity

Fact-check vs FMP

Every headline metric cross-checked against Financial Modeling Prep TTM data. ROIC holds up across the board; two debt/equity figures diverge materially and are flagged.

ROIC — clean. All seven within ~2.6 points of FMP. NVDA (62.9 vs 63.0), TSLA (3.0 vs 3.2), MSFT (21.6 vs 21.0) and AMZN (10.7 vs 9.6) essentially match; META, AAPL and GOOG run ~2 points high, consistent with different invested-capital definitions (lease treatment, cash netting). No red flags.
Debt/Equity — two divergences. AAPL prints 1.52× in-app vs 0.80× at FMP — nearly double, because InsightInvest appears to fold in lease/total liabilities against Apple's very thin book equity, which makes the ratio hypersensitive. AMZN shows 0.37× vs 0.47×, the same lease-driven gap in miniature. Neither is "wrong," but they measure different things — treat the in-app AAPL leverage figure with caution.

Thesis independently confirmed. FMP verifies Amazon's trailing free cash flow is negative — FCF yield −0.09%, EV/FCF −1153×, P/FCF −1109× — which is exactly why the free-cash-flow methods (DFCF-20, H-Model, P/FCF) returned n/a in-app. The "AMZN looks expensive because cash is continuously redeployed into capex and new ventures" argument isn't narrative; it's in the statements. The same fingerprint appears, milder, in TSLA (EV/FCF 211×) and GOOG (EV/FCF 70×).
End of note

Methodology. Composite fair value is a trimmed-mean of ten methods (DCF-20 operating CF, DFCF-20 free CF, DNI-20 net income, H-Model DCF, EV/EBITDA, EV/Revenue, P/FCF, Revenue DCF, PEG-implied, Graham Revised) at a fixed 8% discount rate with method-level growth seeds. Per-stock CAPM-derived WACC differs materially (NVDA 16.6%, TSLA 14.3%, others ~10–11%) and would move several figures if applied. Quality score is a 0–100 six-dimension composite. Metrics cross-checked against Financial Modeling Prep trailing-twelve-month data on Jul 16, 2026.
