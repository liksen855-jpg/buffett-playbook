Investment Thesis — How It Captures the Trend

QQQM tracks the Nasdaq-100 on a market-cap-weighted basis, meaning the fund's exposure to AI/tech leaders scales automatically as those companies compound gains from cloud, AI infrastructure, and semiconductor demand. Investors are not required to forecast which mega-cap wins the AI cycle — the index re-weights toward the winners in real time. The trade-off: concentration, as the top 10 holdings typically represent ~45-50% of the fund.

Broad enough (100 names) to avoid single-stock risk, but sector-concentrated in technology/communications.

Investment Thesis — How It Captures the Trend

AVUV systematically screens small-cap U.S. equities on two academically-supported factors: low relative valuation and high profitability. The fund tilts weight toward companies scoring well on both, rebalancing quarterly with deliberately low turnover (~3-6%). The thesis is structural rather than narrative-driven — it captures a long-documented factor premium rather than betting on a specific theme.

Lowest beta in the basket; broad diversification across 780+ names meaningfully reduces single-stock risk.

Investment Thesis — How It Captures the Trend

XBI's defining design choice is equal-weighting rather than cap-weighting. This deliberately preserves the influence of small and mid-cap biotech names — the companies most likely to deliver outsized returns from a single drug approval, breakthrough trial result, or acquisition. A cap-weighted biotech fund would be dominated by a handful of large pharma names; XBI is built specifically to avoid that and capture the sector's asymmetric upside.

Binary catalysts (FDA decisions, trial readouts) drive sharp, event-based volatility even with sector diversification.

Investment Thesis — How It Captures the Trend

The thesis: AI training and inference are increasingly memory-bandwidth-constrained, so as AI compute scales, memory demand and pricing power scale alongside it. This is a narrow, high-conviction play on one link in the AI supply chain, not a diversified fund with memory as a theme.

Portfolio Construction Notes

  • Core vs. Satellite: QQQM, AVUV, and XBI function as the diversified core of this basket. DRAM and KOID are concentrated, thematic satellites and should be sized accordingly — typically a smaller allocation than the core holdings.
  • Correlation Risk: QQQM, DRAM, and KOID share meaningful overlap through common AI-infrastructure exposure (Micron Technology is a top-3 holding in both QQQM and DRAM). In an AI sentiment reversal, these three may decline together rather than diversifying one another.
  • True Diversifiers: AVUV and XBI carry the lowest correlation to the AI/tech theme within this basket and provide the most genuine ballast if sentiment turns.
  • Suitability: This basket is built for investors who can tolerate significant drawdowns in pursuit of long-run growth — not appropriate for capital that must be preserved or accessed in the near term.
